This is the about page.

Minecraft Minecraft is a sandbox video game developed by Mojang Studios that allows players to explore, create, and survive in a vast block-based world. Since its release in 2011, it has become one of the most popular and best-selling video games ever made. The game is known for its freedom, allowing players to choose their own goals and playstyle rather than following a fixed path.
Every Minecraft world is procedurally generated, meaning no two worlds are exactly the same. Players can discover a wide variety of biomes, including forests, deserts, jungles, oceans, plains, and snowy mountains. These environments contain unique resources, structures, and challenges that encourage exploration and adventure.
One of the core features of Minecraft is its crafting and building system. Players can gather materials such as wood, stone, iron, diamonds, and other resources to create tools, weapons, armor, and countless decorative blocks. With enough imagination, players can build anything from small houses and farms to giant castles, modern cities, and even working computers using redstone.
In Survival Mode, players must manage their health and hunger while protecting themselves from hostile mobs. Creatures such as zombies, skeletons, spiders, and creepers emerge during the night or in dark areas, making preparation and resource management important. Farming, mining, fishing, and animal breeding help players obtain food and materials needed for survival.
Minecraft also features multiple dimensions that expand the gameplay experience. The Nether is a dangerous realm filled with lava, unique resources, and powerful enemies. The End is a mysterious dimension that contains the game's main boss, the Ender Dragon. Defeating the dragon is considered one of the game's major achievements and unlocks access to End Cities and Elytra wings for gliding.
The game supports both single-player and multiplayer experiences. Players can join servers with friends or communities from around the world to participate in minigames, roleplay, survival challenges, and large-scale building projects. This social aspect has helped Minecraft maintain a strong and active player base for many years.
Regular updates continue to introduce new mobs, blocks, biomes, items, and gameplay mechanics. The game's modding community further expands its possibilities with custom content, shaders, texture packs, and entirely new adventures. Thanks to its combination of creativity, exploration, survival, and community-driven content, Minecraft remains one of the most influential and beloved games in gaming history.
